CORE ARTISTS

Sung Hie Ahn

안성희
COMPOSER · PROFESSOR

Sung Hie Ahn is a contemporary composer and professor whose practice occupies a distinct position within ultra-contemporary music and interdisciplinary art. She expands music beyond the realm of sound alone, integrating visuality, space, technology, and narrative into structurally unified artistic forms.

Through her work as a radio host on KBS Classic FM (91.1 MHz), she has connected contemporary music and art with broader public audiences via national broadcasting. Related programs, records, and archives continue to be shared through online platforms.

She serves as the CEO of ARAKOREA.ORG, actively engaging in national brand-making initiatives as well as global environmental and SDGs campaigns. Her experimental art film <DAL, HAE.EYE> (2021) was selected as an award-winning work in the Environmental Category at the World Film Carnival.

She received her Ph.D. from the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign and is currently a professor in the Department of Music at Kangwon National University.

Angel Hong

CREATIVE DIRECTOR · PRODUCER

Angel Hong is a creative director and producer who moves fluidly across music, media, and brand building between North America and Korea. Embedded in Toronto’s evolving music and visual culture, he developed first-hand insight into the ecosystem that shaped a global generation.

He later gained hands-on experience within Hollywood-style production environments, building practical expertise at GEP Productions, a production unit under GE, collaborating with a range of actors, directors, and production teams.

In 2014, he founded TIKOONZ, producing YouTube shows, music videos, and digital media content. Since 2018, he has expanded TIKOONZ into a jewelry brand in Korea, and continues his creative and strategic work through AK ENM and ARAKOREAGROUP.COM.

ASSOCIATED ARTISTS

Maestro Fresh Wes

TORONTO · CANADIAN HIP-HOP PIONEER

Maestro Fresh Wes is widely regarded as a foundational figure in Canadian hip-hop and a cultural anchor of the Toronto scene. He is often described as a “godfather” who mentored artists like Drake and Kardinal Offishall in their formative years, helping establish the generational groundwork before Toronto hip-hop broke fully into the U.S. mainstream.

Drake later became a global superstar who reshaped the modern pop-hip-hop landscape, while Kardinal Offishall helped bridge Canadian culture with North American mainstream visibility. Maestro stands at the origin point of this lineage.

Director X

TORONTO · MUSIC VIDEO & FILM DIRECTOR

Director X is a Toronto-born, world-class music video and film director who has shaped the visual language of contemporary popular music. His work has helped elevate music videos from promotion to global cultural standard—where music, fashion, and city aesthetics converge.

He has collaborated with Kendrick Lamar, Jay-Z, Kanye West, Rihanna, and Usher, and his signature direction remains a defining force in the mainstream today.

Tory Lanez

TORONTO · RAPPER · SINGER · SONGWRITER

Tory Lanez is a Toronto-born artist with an active, ongoing presence in the global mainstream—moving between hip-hop and R&B with a sharp performance identity. He has collaborated with Chris Brown, Meek Mill, Lil Wayne, DaBaby, and Tyga, sustaining influence at the center of contemporary sound and culture.

As a multi-hyphenate who commands recording, performance, and production, he continues to operate as a current-force artist rather than a legacy name.
